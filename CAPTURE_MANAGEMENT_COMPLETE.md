## ðŸ“š Terminology

### **Capture Management**
The process of identifying, qualifying, and pursuing business opportunities before RFP release.

### **P(Win) - Probability of Win**
Assessment of likelihood to win, based on multiple factors:
- Customer relationship strength
- Solution fit
- Competitive position
- Past performance
- Price competitiveness
- Technical approach

### **Win Strategy**
Plan to leverage strengths and address weaknesses to maximize win probability.

### **Win Themes**
Key messages that resonate with customer and differentiate from competitors.

### **Discriminators**
Unique advantages that set you apart from competition.

### **Hot Buttons**
Customer's key concerns, priorities, and decision factors.

### **Ghost/Haunt**
Strategy to highlight competitor weaknesses without naming them.

### **Price-to-Win (PTW)**
Target price that balances competitiveness with profitability.

## ðŸŽ“ Shipley Gate Reviews Explained

### **Opportunity Gate (After Phase 1)**
**Decision**: Bid or No-Bid?
**Criteria**:
- Strategic fit with company goals
- Adequate resources available
- Realistic P(Win) (typically >40%)
- Acceptable risk level
- Customer relationship strength

### **Capture Gate (After Phase 2)**
**Decision**: Continue to proposal phase?
**Criteria**:
- Strong competitive position
- Win strategy validated
- Customer engagement successful
- Teaming partnerships secured
- P(Win) improved (typically >50%)

### **Proposal Gate (After Phase 3)**
**Decision**: Launch proposal effort?
**Criteria**:
- Complete capture plan
- Resources committed
- Price-to-win established
- Solution validated with customer
- Proposal team assembled

### **Submit Gate (End of Phase 4)**
**Decision**: Submit proposal?
**Criteria**:
- All reviews complete
- Quality standards met
- Compliance verified
- Competitive pricing
- Final executive approval
